## 6.3 Systems of Differential Equations
1. If $A\vec{x}=\lambda\vec{x}$, then $\vec{u}(t)=c^{\lambda t}\vec{x}$ will solve $\frac{d\vec{u}}{dt}=A\vec{u}$. Each $\lambda$ and $\vec{x}$ gives a solution $e^{\lambda t}\vec{x}$. 
2. If $A=X\Lambda X^{-1}$ then $u(t)=e^{St}u(0)=Xe^{At}X^{-1}u(0)=c_1e^{\lambda_1t}x_1+\cdots+c_ne^{\lambda_nt}x_n.$
3. $A$ is **stable** and $u(t)\rightarrow 0$ and $e^{At}\rightarrow0$ when all eigenvalues of $A$ have a real part $<0$. 
4. Matrix exponential $e^{At}=I+At+\cdots+(At)^n/n!+\cdots=Xe^{At}X^{-1}$ if $A$ is diagonalizable.
5. Second order equation, first order system: $u''=Bu'+Cu=0$ is equivalent to $\begin{bmatrix}u\\u'\end{bmatrix}'=\begin{bmatrix}0&1\\-C&-B\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}u\\u'\end{bmatrix}$.
