## 1.2 Lengths and Dot Products
1. The "dot product" of $\vec{v}=(1,2)$ and $\vec{w}=(4,5)$ is $\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}=(1)(4)+(2)(5)=4+10=14.$
2. $\vec{v}=(1,3,2)$ and $\vec{w}=(4,-4,4)$ are perpendicular because $\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}=0$. 
3. The length square of $\vec{v}=(1,3,2)$ is $\vec{v}\cdot\vec{v}=1+9+4=14$. The **length**is $\lVert\vec{v}\rVert=\sqrt{14}$.
4. Then $\vec{u}=\frac{\vec{v}}{\lVert\vec{v}\rVert}=\frac{\vec{v}}{\sqrt{14}}=\frac{1}{14}\begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\2\end{bmatrix}$ has length $\lVert\vec{u}\rVert=1$. 
5. The angle $\theta$ between $v$ and $w$ has $\cos{\theta}=\frac{\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}}{\lVert\vec{v}\rVert\:\lVert\vec{w}\rVert}$.
6. The angle between (1,0) and (1,1) has $\cos{\theta}=\frac{1}{(1)(\sqrt{2})}$. That angle is $\theta=45^\circ$.
8. Al angles have $\lvert\cos{\theta}\rvert\le1$. So all vectors have $\lvert\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}\rvert\le\lVert\vec{v}\rVert\:\lVert\vec{w}\rVert$.

---
The **dot product** or **inner product** of $\vec{v}=(v_1,v_2)$ and $\vec{w}=(w_1,w_2)$ is the number $\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}$:$$\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}=v_1w_1+v_2w_2.$$

If two vectors have a dot product of zero they are **perpendicular** vectors.

---
The **length** $\lVert\vec{v}\rVert$ of a vector $\vec{v}$ is the square root of $\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}$:$$\textbf{length}=\lVert\vec{v}\rVert=\sqrt{\vec{v}\cdot\vec{v}}=(v^2_1+v^2_2+\dots+v^2_n)^{1/2}.$$

---
A **unit vector** $\vec{u} is a vector whose length equals one. Thus, $\vec{u}\cdot\vec{u}=1$. 

The vector $\vec{u}=\frac{\vec{v}}{\lVert\vec{v}\rVert}$ is a univer vetor in the directon of $\vec{v}$. 

---
The dot product is $\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}=0$ when $\vec{v}$ is perpendicular to $\vec{w}$. 

Unit vectors $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{U}$ at angle $\theta$ have $\vec{v}\cdot\vec{U}=\cos{\theta}$.

---
**Cosine Formula** If $\vec{v}$ and $\vec{w}$ are nonzero vectors, then $\frac{\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}}{\lVert\vec{v}\rVert\:\lVert\vec{w}\rVert}=\cos{\theta}$. 

**Schwarz Inequality** $\lvert\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}\le\lVert\vec{v}\rVert\:\lVert\vec{w}\rVert$.

**Triangle Ineuqality** $\lVert\vec{v}+\vec{w}\rVert\le\lVert\vec{v}\rVert+\lVert\vec{w}\rVert$.
