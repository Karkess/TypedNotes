## 3.5 - Dimensions of the Four Subspaces

1. The column space $\mathbb{C}(A)$ and the row space $\mathbb{C}(A^\text{T})$ both have *dimension $r$* (the rank of $A$).
2. The nullspace $\mathbb{N}$ has *dimension* $n-r$. The left nullspace $\mathbb{N}(A^\text{T})$ has dimension $m-r$. 
3. Elimination produces the bases for the row space and nullspace of $A$; They are the same as for $R$. 
4. Elimination often changes the column space and left nullspace (but dimensions don't change). 
5. **Rank one matrices**: $A=\vec{u}\,\vec{v}\,^\text{T}=$column times row: $\mathbb{C}(A)$ has basis $\vec{u},\mathbb{C}(A^\text{T})$ has basis $\vec{v}$.

---
The main theorem in this chapter connects **rank** and **dimension**. The **rank** in a matrix is the number of pivots. The **dimension** of a subspace is the number of vectors in its basis. 

---
**Four Fundamental Subpsaces**
1. The ***row space*** is $\mathbb{N}(A^\text{T})$, a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^n$. 
2. The ***column space*** is $\mathbb{N}(A)$, a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^m$. 
3. The ***nullspace*** is $\mathbb{N}(A)$, a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^n$. 
4. The ***left nullspace*** is $\mathbb{N}(A^\text{T})$, a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^m$. 

---
The row space and the column space have the same dimension $r$, which is the *rank* of the matrix. $$\mathbb{N}(A)\text{ and }\mathbb{N}(A^\text{T})\text{ have dimensions }n-r\text{ and }m-r\text{, to make up the full }n\text{ and }m.$$

The nonzero rows and the pivot columns both form the bases for their respective subspaces. 

---
So the left nullspaces are forbidden solutions, the nullspace is zero solutions. In physics the nullspace would be solving for invariants and the left nullspace forbidden solutions, such as forbidden transitions in quantum mechanics. This is abstracted to the operator view, but remains the same.

---
**The big picture**
![image](https://hackmd.io/_uploads/rkdJA6CXZx.png)

$A$ reduces to $R$: $R=\mathrm{rref}(A)$.

1. $A$ has the same row space as $R$. Same dimension $r$ and same basis. 
2. The column space of $A$ has dimension $r$. The column rank equals the row rank. *Rank Theorem: The number of independent columns = The number of independent rows.*
3. $A$ has the same nullspace as $R$. Same dimension $n-r$ and same basis. $$(\text{dimension of column space})+(\text{dimension of nullspace})=\text{dimension of }\mathbb{R}^n.$$
4. The left nullspace of $A$ (the nullspace of $A^\text{T}$) has dimension $m-r$. 

---
**Fundamental Theorem of Linear Algebra (Part 1)**
*The column space and row space both have dimension $r$*
*The nullspaces have dimension $n-r$ and $m-r$*. 

---
**Every rank one matrix is one column times one row**$$A=\vec{u}\,\vec{v}\,^\text{T}$$

---
**Rank Two Matrices = Rank One plus Rank One**

**Review of Key Ideas**
1. The $r$ pivot rows of $R$ are a basis for the row spaces of $R$ and $A$ (same space).
2. The $r$ pivot columns of $A$ are a basis for its column space $\mathbb{C}(A)$. 
3. The $n-r$ special columns are a basis for the nullspaces of $A$ and $R$ (same space).
4. If $EA=R$, the last $m-r$ rows of $E$ are a basis for the left nullspace of $A$.
